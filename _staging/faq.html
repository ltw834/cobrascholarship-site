Eligibility & Requirements

Youth ages 4-26 living in North Dallas/Plano area who demonstrate financial need are eligible. We specifically look for:

  • Free/reduced lunch program participation
  • SNAP, Medicaid, or housing assistance
  • Single-parent households
  • Other documented financial hardship

Applicants must also commit to regular attendance and positive behavior.

No prior experience is required! Our program is designed to welcome complete beginners. Many of our most successful scholars started with zero martial arts background.

Our partner academies specialize in age-appropriate instruction that meets each student where they are.

Currently, we focus on the North Dallas and Plano communities where we have established partnerships with local academies.

However, we encourage you to apply if you're in nearby areas like Richardson, Allen, or McKinney. We evaluate each application individually and may make exceptions based on circumstances and travel feasibility.

Application Process

The application process is straightforward:

  • Complete our online application form
  • Provide basic information about your situation and goals
  • Submit any required documentation
  • Participate in a brief interview if selected

The entire process typically takes 1-2 weeks from application to decision.

Required documents include:

  • Proof of age (birth certificate or school ID)
  • Proof of residence in service area
  • Documentation of financial need (if selected for final review)
  • Parent/guardian consent (if under 18)

Don't worry if you don't have everything immediately - we can work with you to gather necessary documents.

Most applications are processed within 1-2 weeks. Here's the typical timeline:

  • Application submission: Day 1
  • Initial review: Days 2-5
  • Interview (if selected): Days 6-10
  • Final decision: Days 10-14

We notify all applicants of decisions regardless of outcome.

Program Details

Our full scholarships include:

  • Complete monthly tuition coverage
  • Uniform (gi) and basic gear
  • Competition entry fees
  • One-on-one mentorship support
  • Life skills workshops
  • Transportation assistance when needed

The total value is typically $2,000-3,500 per year per scholar.

We expect scholars to attend 2-3 classes per week consistently. This level of commitment ensures:

  • Steady skill development and progression
  • Strong relationships with instructors and peers
  • Maximum benefit from the program investment

We understand life happens - occasional absences are fine, but regular attendance is essential for success.

Scholarships are renewable every 6 months based on:

  • Regular attendance and participation
  • Positive attitude and respect for instructors/peers
  • Continued financial need
  • Progress toward personal goals

We encourage long-term participation and have scholars who've been with us for 2+ years.

We work with you to find the best fit based on:

  • Geographic proximity to your home/school
  • Schedule compatibility
  • Academy culture and teaching style
  • Age-appropriate program availability

We partner with multiple high-quality academies and will match you with the best option for your situation.

Support & Resources

Every scholar is paired with a dedicated mentor who provides:

  • Academic support and tutoring
  • Goal setting and achievement planning
  • Life skills development
  • Career and college guidance
  • Personal development coaching
  • Someone to talk to about challenges and successes

Mentors are carefully screened volunteers with backgrounds in education, martial arts, or youth development.

We understand transportation can be a barrier and offer several solutions:

  • Carpools with other families when possible
  • Public transit support and training
  • Volunteer driver program for special circumstances
  • Academy location matching to minimize travel

Let us know your transportation situation during the application process - we'll work with you to find a solution.

Family engagement is crucial to our success. We provide:

  • Regular progress updates and check-ins
  • Family events and academy visits
  • Parent resources and education
  • Connection to community support services when needed
  • Open communication with program staff

We believe supporting the whole family creates the best outcomes for our scholars.
